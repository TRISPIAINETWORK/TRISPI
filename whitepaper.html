§ 01

Abstract

TRISPI is a next-generation, AI-powered blockchain network designed for the Web4 era. It introduces Proof of Intelligence (PoI) — a novel consensus mechanism that evaluates validator contributions through a multidimensional AI scoring model rather than raw computational power or staked capital alone. TRISPI combines post-quantum cryptography (Ed25519 hybrid with NIST-standard Dilithium3 lattice-based signatures), a dynamic EIP-1559-style burn mechanism with Bitcoin-style halving, and a three-layer service architecture (Python AI Engine, Go Consensus Node, Rust Core Bridge) to deliver unprecedented security, fairness, and intelligence to decentralised computation.

The native token TRP has a fixed genesis supply of 50,000,000 units with a deflationary model driven by fee burning, halving epochs every 500,000 blocks, and AI-weighted block rewards. The network is governed by a dual-layer system combining on-chain referendum voting with AI-mediated proposal scoring, ensuring fair and Sybil-resistant decision making.

Core thesis: A blockchain network that uses machine intelligence to evaluate and reward honest participation — making consensus smarter, more efficient, and quantum-safe.

§ 02

Introduction & Vision

The blockchain industry has evolved through three distinct generations: Bitcoin introduced decentralised, censorship-resistant value transfer (Web3 money); Ethereum added programmable smart contracts (Web3 compute); and now the Web4 era demands that blockchain networks become intelligent infrastructure — adaptive, quantum-resistant, and capable of autonomous decision-making.

TRISPI is purpose-built for Web4. Where legacy networks reward whoever spends the most electricity (PoW) or stakes the most capital (PoS), TRISPI rewards intelligence: nodes that contribute meaningfully verified computation, maintain high peer reliability, and build a proven reputation of honest participation receive proportionally higher block rewards.

Design Principles

  • Intelligence-first consensus — AI scoring drives validator selection and reward weighting.
  • Quantum-safe from day one — every block carries a Dilithium3 lattice signature in addition to Ed25519.
  • Decentralised governance — no single entity controls protocol upgrades; changes require community supermajority + AI vetting.
  • Deflationary token model — EIP-1559 burns reduce circulating supply over time; halving further constrains emission.
  • Modular architecture — three independent services (Python, Go, Rust) can be upgraded independently without hard forks.
  • Openness — fully open-source; validators run commodity hardware; no ASIC advantage.

§ 03

Problem Statement

3.1 The Energy Problem

Bitcoin's Proof of Work consumes more electricity annually than many mid-sized nations. This energy expenditure is inherently wasteful — the work performed exists solely to make attacks expensive, producing no useful computation beyond the consensus itself.

3.2 The Plutocracy Problem

Proof of Stake systems concentrate power in the hands of the largest token holders. Wealth begets influence: large stakers earn proportionally more rewards, accumulate more tokens, and gain disproportionate governance weight. This reproduces traditional financial power structures within decentralised systems.

3.3 The Quantum Threat

Current blockchain networks rely on elliptic-curve cryptography (ECDSA, Ed25519) which is vulnerable to Shor's algorithm running on a sufficiently powerful quantum computer. NIST estimates that quantum computers capable of breaking 256-bit ECC could exist within 10–15 years. No major blockchain network is quantum-resistant today.

3.4 The Fairness Problem

Neither PoW nor PoS rewards good behaviour — they reward resources. A validator that consistently provides accurate data, maintains high uptime, and contributes to network health earns no more than an equivalent validator that performs minimally.

Summary: The blockchain industry needs a consensus mechanism that is energy-efficient, fair to participants of all economic scales, quantum-resistant, and rewards genuine contribution rather than raw resource ownership.

§ 04

Network Architecture

TRISPI's architecture separates concerns across three specialised services that communicate via authenticated HTTP APIs and shared on-disk state. This modularity allows each component to be upgraded, scaled, or replaced independently without requiring a hard fork of the others.

Figure 2 — Three-layer service architecture. Each layer communicates via authenticated HTTP; failures in one layer do not crash others.

4.1 Python AI Service (Port 8000)

The Python layer is the brain of the network. It runs the Proof of Intelligence scoring engine, manages the wallet and balance ledger, handles REST and GraphQL APIs for clients, coordinates the AI miner fleet, and persists the canonical blockchain state to disk. It is implemented with FastAPI + Uvicorn for high-throughput async request handling.

  • Proof of Intelligence (PoI) validator scoring with NumPy
  • EIP-1559 dynamic gas pricing and burn accounting
  • BIP39 mnemonic wallet derivation (Ed25519)
  • GraphQL API (Strawberry) for rich client queries
  • AI fleet management: 1,000+ miner nodes across 10 global regions
  • State persistence with Merkle-style state root tracking

4.2 Go Consensus Node (Port 8084)

The Go layer is the heartbeat of the network. It runs the block-mining loop, applies the Practical Byzantine Fault Tolerance (PBFT) protocol for finality, manages the staking ledger, and handles peer-to-peer block propagation using libp2p with both TCP and QUIC transports. It also queries the Rust bridge for Dilithium3 signatures on every block.

  • Mining loop with configurable target difficulty (leading-zero hash)
  • AI score integration: validators are weighted by their PoI score
  • PBFT prepare/commit protocol for Byzantine fault tolerance (f < n/3)
  • libp2p mDNS local discovery + DHT global peer discovery
  • Staking ledger: validators must stake TRP to participate
  • HTTP callback to Python on every confirmed block

4.3 Rust Core Bridge (Port 6000)

The Rust layer is the security fortress of the network. It provides post-quantum signing services via Dilithium3 (CRYSTALS-Dilithium, NIST PQC round-3 standard), hosts a WASM virtual machine for contract execution, and provides an EVM compatibility adapter for Ethereum tool integration.

  • Dilithium3 lattice-based signature generation and verification
  • Ed25519 / Dilithium3 hybrid signing (classical + quantum-safe)
  • WASM VM: secure sandboxed contract execution
  • EVM adapter: Ethereum-compatible transaction processing

Proof of Intelligence Consensus

Proof of Intelligence (PoI) is TRISPI's original consensus mechanism. Instead of evaluating validators by energy expenditure or staked capital, PoI evaluates them across four measurable dimensions of network contribution, computed by the AI scoring engine.

5.1 AI Score Formula

score = 0.45 × reputation + 0.30 × peer_score + 0.15 × tx_throughput + 0.10 × consecutive_successes

score ∈ [0.60, 0.99] (clamped)

Peer Score30%Quality of P2P connections and relay speedConnected peer count × average relay latency inverse

Consecutive Successes10%Streak of consecutive valid block proposals without slashingConsecutiveSuccesses counter, reset on any invalid block

5.2 Validator Selection

At each round, the Go consensus node computes a weighted selection probability for each registered validator: 

P(validator_i) = AI_score_i / Σ AI_scores_all_active_validators

A validator with score 0.90 is therefore 50% more likely to be selected as block proposer than one with score 0.60. This creates a strong incentive for validators to maintain high quality of service rather than simply accumulating more hardware.

§ 06

Post-Quantum Cryptography

TRISPI uses a hybrid classical + post-quantum signature scheme on every block, providing security against both classical and quantum adversaries. No blockchain in production today achieves this standard.

6.1 Ed25519 (Classical Layer)

Ed25519 is the Twisted Edwards Curve signature scheme over the Curve25519 field. It provides 128-bit classical security and is extremely fast — over 70,000 signatures/second on modern CPUs. Ed25519 signatures are 64 bytes and public keys 32 bytes. 

Private key: 32 bytes (random scalar)
Public key:  32 bytes (point on Ed25519 curve)
Signature:   64 bytes (R + S values)
Security:    128-bit classical / 0-bit quantum (Shor-vulnerable)

6.2 Dilithium3 (Quantum Layer)

Dilithium3 is NIST's standardised (FIPS 204 draft) lattice-based digital signature algorithm based on the hardness of the Module Learning With Errors (MLWE) problem. It is designed to resist attacks by quantum computers running Shor's or Grover's algorithms. 

Public key:  1,952 bytes
Private key: 4,000 bytes
Signature:   3,293 bytes
Security:    128-bit quantum / 180-bit classical (NIST Level 3)

6.3 Hybrid Block Signing Protocol

Figure 4 — Hybrid block signing: Ed25519 (classical, fast) + Dilithium3 (quantum-safe). Both signatures are verified by receiving peers.

6.4 Wallet Address Derivation

TRISPI wallet addresses are derived from BIP39 mnemonics using a TRISPI-specific quantum derivation:

seed         = PBKDF2-HMAC-SHA512(mnemonic, "mnemonic", 2048)
combined     = seed + path_bytes("m/44'/888'/0'/0/0")
quantum_priv = SHA256(HMAC-SHA512("TRISPI quantum", combined)[:32])
quantum_pub  = Ed25519.getPublicKey(quantum_priv)
trp_address  = "trp1" + SHA256(quantum_pub).hex()[:38]

All wallets are compatible with both the TRISPI native chain and the EVM-compatible layer. EVM addresses are derived from the same seed using standard BIP44 path m/44'/60'/0'/0/0.

7.3 EIP-1559 Burn Mechanism

Every transaction on TRISPI pays a base fee determined dynamically by the network's congestion level (EIP-1559 algorithm). Of this fee:

  • 70% is burned — permanently removed from circulation, making TRP deflationary under load
  • 30% is credited to the current block's validator as a priority tip

Figure 5 — EIP-1559 fee split: 70% burned deflationary pressure, 30% validator tip

7.4 Dynamic Supply Model

current_supply(t) = GENESIS_SUPPLY + Σ block_rewards(0..t) − Σ burned_fees(0..t)

Where:
  block_reward(epoch) = 10 / 2^epoch   (halving)
  burned_fee(tx)      = base_fee × 0.70

7.5 Deflationary Inflection Point

As the network grows and transaction volume increases, the burn rate from fees will eventually exceed new issuance from block rewards. This deflationary inflection point makes TRP a net-deflationary asset above a critical daily transaction volume threshold — analogous to Ethereum's EIP-1559 "ultrasound money" model, but combined with a Bitcoin-style hard supply ceiling from halving.

Key insight: TRISPI achieves the best of both Bitcoin (hard cap via halving) and Ethereum (dynamic burns via EIP-1559), creating a token that is both scarce and deflationary in high-activity conditions.

§ 09

Smart Contract Engine

TRISPI supports three contract execution environments, enabling developers to choose the best tool for their use case:

9.1 EVM Compatibility Layer

The Rust bridge includes an EVM adapter that makes TRISPI fully compatible with Ethereum tooling: Solidity contracts, MetaMask wallet, Hardhat/Foundry deployment, and all standard ERC-20/721/1155 token standards. Developers can port Ethereum dApps to TRISPI without rewriting a single line of Solidity.

9.2 WASM Virtual Machine

For high-performance contracts, TRISPI provides a WebAssembly (WASM) VM executed by the Rust Wasmtime runtime. WASM contracts can be written in any language that compiles to WASM (Rust, Go, C, AssemblyScript) and execute at near-native speed with deterministic gas metering.

9.3 Hybrid Bridge

A unique bridge layer allows EVM contracts and WASM contracts to call each other via a standardised cross-VM message passing protocol. This enables developers to write performance-sensitive components in WASM while maintaining full EVM compatibility for wallet and DEX integration.

§ 10

P2P Network Protocol

TRISPI's peer-to-peer layer is built on libp2p — the same networking stack used by Ethereum 2.0, IPFS, Filecoin, and Polkadot. This provides battle-tested, high-performance peer discovery and block propagation.

10.1 Transport Protocols

  • TCP/IP — reliable transport for block and transaction propagation (port 50052)
  • QUIC — low-latency UDP-based transport for real-time consensus messages
  • mDNS — automatic local network peer discovery (no bootstrap required on LAN)
  • DHT (Kademlia) — global decentralised peer discovery via IPFS bootstrap nodes

10.2 Block Propagation

When a block is mined, it is gossiped to all connected peers within one RTT. Each peer independently verifies:

  1. Block hash meets target difficulty (leading zeros)
  2. Ed25519 signature valid against proposer's registered public key
  3. Dilithium3 signature valid (quantum layer)
  4. AI score of proposer meets minimum threshold
  5. All transactions are valid (no double-spends, sufficient balances)

10.3 PBFT Finality

After block propagation, TRISPI applies Practical Byzantine Fault Tolerance (PBFT) to achieve fast, deterministic finality. A block is considered final when at least 2/3 of active validators have sent a signed COMMIT message: 

Finality requires: ⌊(2 × n) / 3⌋ + 1 COMMIT messages
  where n = number of active validators

Example: 10 validators → 7 COMMITs required for finality
         31 validators → 21 COMMITs required for finality

This Byzantine fault tolerance guarantees safety as long as fewer than 1/3 of validators are malicious or offline simultaneously.

§ 11

Decentralised Governance

TRISPI uses a DualGov governance system that combines on-chain token-weighted voting with AI-mediated proposal analysis to prevent plutocratic capture and low-quality proposals.

11.1 Proposal Lifecycle

1

Submission

Any account holding ≥ 100 TRP may submit a governance proposal with a description, code changes (if applicable), and rationale. A 10 TRP submission bond is required (returned if proposal passes, burned if rejected).

2

AI Pre-screening

The AI Contract Auditor and Trust Registry assess the proposal for spam indicators, duplicate detection, and technical soundness. Proposals scoring below 0.40 are auto-rejected before voting.

3

Voting Period (7 days)

Token holders vote YES / NO / ABSTAIN. Voting power = TRP balance × PoI score (preventing pure plutocracy — high AI-score validators have amplified votes).

4

Quorum Check

Proposal passes if: (1) ≥ 33% of circulating supply participates, (2) ≥ 60% of votes are YES, (3) AI pre-screening score ≥ 0.60.

5

Execution

Passed proposals enter a 48-hour timelock before execution. During timelock, security review and emergency veto by the validator set (supermajority of 80%) is possible.

11.2 Treasury Governance

The 20,000,000 TRP Treasury allocation is governed exclusively through DualGov proposals. No individual or team can unilaterally access treasury funds. All treasury disbursements are on-chain, auditable, and subject to the full governance lifecycle.

12.2 Key Security Properties

  • Liveness: Network continues to produce blocks as long as > 2/3 of validators are online and honest.
  • Safety: Two conflicting blocks cannot both be finalised if < 1/3 of validators are Byzantine.
  • Quantum safety: Dilithium3 provides 128-bit post-quantum security per NIST PQC standardisation.
  • Confidentiality: Transactions are public by default (transparent ledger). Private transactions via zero-knowledge proofs are planned for v2.0.
  • Non-repudiation: Dual signature (Ed25519 + Dilithium3) makes it computationally infeasible to deny authorship of any block.

12.3 Validator Whitelisting

During the genesis phase (first 100,000 blocks), the validator set is whitelisted to prevent Sybil attacks during bootstrapping. After the bootstrapping phase ends, any address meeting the minimum stake requirement and AI score threshold may join as a validator permissionlessly.

Conclusion

By unifying artificial intelligence, post-quantum cryptography, and a fair merit-based consensus mechanism into a single coherent protocol, TRISPI addresses the three most critical challenges facing blockchain networks today: energy waste, unfair power concentration, and quantum vulnerability.

TRISPI is not just a blockchain — it is a platform for the intelligent, quantum-safe, decentralised economy of Web4.
